How I Think

How I make decisions
in AI product design.

From shipping AI workflows where trust, model uncertainty, and business speed must align.

01

"AI should reduce effort, not remove user agency."

Hybrid flows let users accept, edit, or override AI. Autocomplete without control fails in high-stakes work.

02

"Good UX is usually about sequencing information."

Drop-offs follow uncertainty. I sequence context, confidence cues, and actions so decisions feel informed.

03

"Ambiguity gets resolved through states, not screens."

Query states, empty states, and recovery paths come first. Failed model responses still need a clear next action.

04

"I push back when decisions are assumption-led."

I turn assumptions into testable bets. Early evidence prevents costly post-launch redesigns.

05

"Metrics and behavior both matter."

Metrics show where to look; user conversations show what to fix.

06

"Cross-functional clarity beats isolated design polish."

I align with PMs and engineers so design reflects constraints and priorities—not slide-deck ideals.
